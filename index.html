Materials Required:
1. Hydrogen Peroxide (H2O2)
2. Potassium Permanganate (KMnO4)
3. Gloves
4. Small bottle (preferably glass or metal, as the reaction heats up)
5. Liquid soap
6. Water
7. Optional: food colours/dye



Step 1: Prepare for Safety
Put on gloves to ensure safety during the experiment.

Step 2: Hydrogen Peroxide Mixture
Take a small bottle, preferably glass or metal, and pour the given Hydrogen Peroxide (H2O2) solution into it.
Add a some amount of liquid soap to the hydrogen peroxide in the bottle and stir it well.

Step 3: Potassium Permanganate solution
In a separate cup, add about 5-10 ml of water and add Potassium Permanganate (KMnO4) to the water, creating a solution.

Step 4: Mix the Solutions
Watch as the chemical reaction occurs, resulting in the formation of foam, known as Elephant Toothpaste.

Step 5: Observe the Reaction
When ready, pour the potassium permanganate solution into the hydrogen peroxide and soap mixture in the bottle.

Step 6: Optional: Add Color:
To add a colorful touch, consider introducing dye or food coloring to the walls inside the bottle before adding the solutions.

Hydrogen Peroxide Breaks Down:
Hydrogen peroxide is a liquid that breaks down into water and oxygen gas naturally.

Potassium Permanganate Speeds it Up:
We use a tiny amount of potassium permanganate to make this breakdown happen faster. It's like giving the reaction a little kick.

Foam Shows Oxygen Release:
When hydrogen peroxide breaks down, it releases a lot of oxygen gas. The soap we add helps trap this oxygen, creating a big, bubbly foam.
